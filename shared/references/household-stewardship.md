# Household Stewardship — Detailed Reference

## Time-Horizon Matching Table

| Horizon | Purpose | Appropriate Asset |
|---------|---------|------------------|
| 0-1 years | Emergency fund, near-term expenses | High-yield savings, money market, T-bills |
| 1-5 years | Car purchase, home down payment, known expenses | Short-term bonds, CDs, I-Bonds, bond ladder |
| 5-15 years | Education, major purchases | Balanced portfolio (60/40 to 70/30), 529 plans |
| 15-30 years | Retirement accumulation | Equity-heavy portfolio (80/20 to 90/10) |
| 30+ years | Multigenerational wealth, legacy | Equity-dominant, real estate, private business |

## Tax-Advantaged Account Hierarchy

Deploy in order of efficiency:

### 1. HSA (Health Savings Account)
Triple-tax-advantaged: deductible contribution, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawal for medical expenses. The steward's move: contribute max, pay medical expenses out of pocket, let the HSA compound for decades, reimburse yourself in retirement. No time limit on reimbursement.

### 2. Employer 401(k) Match
Guaranteed 50-100% return on contributed dollars. Take every dollar of match before anything else.

### 3. Roth IRA / Roth 401(k)
Tax-free growth and withdrawal. Most valuable in low-income years (career transitions, sabbaticals, early career). Execute Roth conversions strategically when taxable income is abnormally low.

### 4. 529 Plans
State tax deduction (in many states) + tax-free growth for education. Overfunding risk manageable: unused 529 funds can roll into beneficiary's Roth IRA (up to $35K lifetime, subject to annual Roth contribution limits).

### 5. Traditional 401(k) / IRA
Deductible now, taxed later. Beneficial when current marginal rate exceeds expected retirement rate.

### 6. Donor-Advised Funds (DAFs)
Contribute appreciated stock (avoid capital gains entirely), take the deduction in a high-income year, distribute grants to charities over time. For a Catholic household practicing tithing, a DAF funded with appreciated shares is dramatically more efficient than cash donations.

### Roth Conversion Ladder
Critical multi-year strategy: in early retirement or low-income years, systematically convert traditional IRA to Roth, paying tax at low marginal rates, to reduce future Required Minimum Distributions and create a tax-free inheritance for the next generation.

## Insurance Framework

### Umbrella Liability
$1-5M of coverage costs $200-500/year. Protects the household's entire net worth from lawsuits. The most underused financial tool available.

### Term Life
Pure protection of human capital. Amount = present value of future income that dependents rely on. Unnecessary once financial assets replace human capital (financial independence). Never whole life or universal life — these are poor investments wrapped in insurance products. The rare exception: ultra-high-net-worth estate planning via irrevocable life insurance trust (ILIT).

### Disability Insurance
More important than life insurance for working-age adults — far more likely to be disabled than to die during earning years. Long-term disability covering 60-70% of income is the target.

### Long-Term Care Insurance
A single LTC event ($100K+/year) can consume a generation's accumulated wealth. Becomes relevant for the older generation. Plan for it before it's needed.

## Estate Planning Structures

### Revocable Living Trust
Avoids probate (public, slow, expensive). Maintains privacy. Ensures seamless management if grantor becomes incapacitated. Every household with meaningful assets should have one.

### Beneficiary Designations
Override wills. Audit annually. Retirement accounts, life insurance, and TOD (transfer on death) accounts pass by designation, not by will. Mismatched designations are one of the most common estate planning failures.

### Irrevocable Trusts
Remove assets from taxable estate. Tradeoff: loss of control. Relevant when estate approaches federal exemption threshold.

### Generation-Skipping Trust (GST)
Preserves wealth across multiple generations by avoiding estate tax at each generational transfer. "Dynasty trust" in states without rule against perpetuities.

### Family Limited Partnership (FLP)
Allows senior generation to transfer assets at valuation discount (lack of marketability, minority interest discounts of 20-35%) while retaining management control.

## Asset Class Details

### Real Estate

**Direct ownership roles:**
- Cash flow engine: rental income, evaluated on cap rate (NOI / purchase price). 6%+ cap rate in stable market is threshold.
- Inflation hedge: rents and replacement costs rise with inflation. Asset reprices naturally.
- Leverage vehicle: 30-year fixed-rate mortgage is extraordinary — borrow at fixed nominal rate while inflation erodes real cost.

**REITs:** Liquid real estate exposure but trade with equity-market correlation short-term. Useful for sector exposure (cell towers, data centers, farmland) impractical via direct ownership.

**Farmland:** ~10% annual returns (income + appreciation), low equity correlation, built-in inflation protection.

### Fixed Income

**Treasury bonds:** Not an "investment" but a parking place for purchasing power with sovereign guarantee.

**I-Bonds / TIPS:** The only instruments contractually guaranteeing real (inflation-adjusted) returns. I-Bonds up to $10K/year per person. Tax-deferred, state-tax-exempt, zero downside risk.

**Municipal bonds:** Relevant only after tax-equivalent yield analysis. A 4% muni at 32% federal bracket = ~5.9% taxable equivalent.

**Corporate bonds:** Investment-grade offers modest premium. High-yield is equity risk disguised as fixed income.

**Bond ladders:** Individual bonds held to maturity at staggered intervals (1, 2, 3, 5, 7, 10 years). Eliminates interest rate risk entirely and creates predictable liquidity.

### Private Business

Highest-returning asset class but extreme concentration risk. Key distinctions:
- Operating a business (human capital commitment = cost) vs investing in one (information advantage required)
- Private equity funds: "2 and 20" fee structure. Median returns roughly match public equities after fees. Top quartile outperforms — but access to top quartile is itself a scarce asset.
- Direct private investment rational only where you have information advantage the public market cannot replicate.

### Gold and Precious Metals

Gold is monetary insurance, not an investment. No cash flow. Centuries-old store of value during loss of confidence in fiat currency. 3-5% allocation to physical gold (not ETFs, not mining stocks) as portfolio insurance against tail risks. Hope never to need it.

Broad commodities (oil, copper, agricultural) are not appropriate for household portfolios — require futures contracts, negative roll yield, fundamentally speculative.

### Alternatives — Smell Test

**Legitimate:** Tax liens (secured by property, require local expertise), revenue-based financing / direct lending, royalties (music, mineral, patent).

**Marketing dressed as alternatives:** Hedge funds (median has underperformed 60/40 for 15+ years after fees), most "structured products" (complexity is the fee in disguise).

## The Primary Residence

A nuanced view:
- **Is an asset** — has value, provides imputed rent, typically appreciates with inflation
- **Is not an investment** — no cash flow, 6% transaction costs, 1-2% annual maintenance, "return" consumed by living in it
- US housing prices adjusted for inflation were essentially flat from 1890 to 1997 (Shiller)
- A consumption asset with a savings component: locks in housing costs via fixed mortgage, forces savings through amortization, significant tax advantages ($250K/$500K capital gains exclusion)
- Never count home equity as the "investment portfolio." Build financial assets in addition to home equity.

## Dalio's Four Economic Environments

| Environment | Favored Assets |
|------------|---------------|
| Rising growth + rising inflation | Commodities, TIPS, real estate |
| Rising growth + falling inflation | Equities |
| Falling growth + rising inflation (stagflation) | Commodities, gold, TIPS |
| Falling growth + falling inflation | Long-term Treasury bonds |

The steward ensures the household has some exposure in each quadrant without adopting Dalio's exact allocations.
